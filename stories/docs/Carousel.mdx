# Carousel

A swipeable, keyboard-navigable slideshow built on Embla. `components/ui/carousel.tsx`
wires Embla to design-system Prev/Next `Button`s and exposes slots, styled through the
Apple-language tokens. Motion and dragging come from Embla; the chrome is token-driven.

## Anatomy

```
[‹]  ┌──────┐ ┌──────┐ ┌──────┐  [›]
     │  1   │ │  2   │ │  3   │
     └──────┘ └──────┘ └──────┘
  │   CarouselItem (basis sets per-view)
  └─ CarouselPrevious / CarouselNext (Buttons)
```

- **Carousel** — the Embla root; `orientation` `horizontal` (default) or `vertical`.
- **CarouselContent / CarouselItem** — the track and slides; set item `basis-*` for multi-per-view.
- **CarouselPrevious / CarouselNext** — icon Buttons; auto-disable at the ends.

## Usage

- **Do** use a Carousel for browsable, equal-weight items (gallery, cards, testimonials).
- **Do** keep Prev/Next visible; don't rely on swipe alone (desktop has no swipe).
- **Do** set item `basis` to control how many slides show at once.
- **Don't** hide critical content in later slides — many users never advance.
- **Don't** hardcode colors — the arrows are Buttons; slides are your own content/Cards.

## Token mapping

| Part | Tokens |
|---|---|
| Prev / Next | `--button-outline-*`, `--button-focus-ring` |
| Slides | your content (often `Card` → `--card-*`) |

Carousel adds no tokens of its own — it composes Button and your slide content.

## Accessibility (WCAG 2.2 AA)

- Prev/Next are real Buttons with `aria-label`s and visible focus rings; they disable at the bounds.
- The region is keyboard-navigable (arrow keys move slides).
- Embla respects pointer and keyboard; pair with pause controls if you add autoplay.
- Don't convey meaning by slide position alone — each slide stands on its own.
